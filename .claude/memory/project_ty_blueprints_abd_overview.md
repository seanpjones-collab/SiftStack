## 8. Graduation Criteria

### A → B
- First deal closes (proves the system works on free data)
- Operator gains capital but acquires a W-2 / time constraint
- Pipeline outgrows what one person can dial during business hours

### A → C
- First deal closes
- Operator discovers a niche with information-asymmetry margin (probate, tax foreclosure, land, commercial)
- Prefers depth-and-margin over volume; wants to remain the closer

### B → D
- Team has a Closer who is overflowing
- Need 2nd Lead Manager / 2nd Closer pair
- Marketing dollars are flowing but cost-per-deal is opaque → need KPI dashboard
- Operator can't yet step out of day-to-day; needs a Sales Manager hire

### C → D (rare path)
- Specialist deliberately decides to pivot from depth to volume — usually a different business
- More commonly, Specialists stay at C indefinitely (Quintin Givery: $458K in 7 mo with 2-person team, no scale-up)

### D → "Business You Can Sell"
- All 4 pillars green (Marketing / Sales / Operations / KPIs each ≥4/5)
- Sales Manager hired with override; Owner works ON not IN
- Cost-per-deal under $3K, 3–5+ deals/mo predictable
- Multi-market expansion validated through Market Finder

### Hard stop — when NOT to graduate
- Blueprint D explicitly warns: if your 4-pillar Company Audit is low, scaling **amplifies** problems instead of solving them. Drop back to A to rebuild fundamentals.

## 9. Why Sean Is on C, Not A / B / D

Cross-reference: `project_ty_blueprint_c_specialist.md` and Sean's `CLAUDE.md` defaults section.

### Why not A (Launchpad)
- Sean is already past the first-deal stage and operates a working FTM Apify pipeline (6 OH scrapers, 9 AM ET daily, since 2026-04-22).
- Tech stack already runs $0–$665/mo on the Specialist Full Stack ladder, not $0/mo Essentials.
- A is for pre-deal solo operators whose constraint is fear and analysis paralysis — Sean's constraint is the opposite (operating an automation stack and an existing book).

### Why not B (Optimizer)
- Sean is **not** time-poor due to a W-2; his constraint is depth/margin, not "phone rings during meetings."
- B's mandatory 2:1 caller-to-Lead-Manager ratio implies a 4-person team. Sean's plan is a single Data Manager hire — same as Quintin Givery's $458K/7-mo lean team.
- B's stacked-niche fast-start ($499/mo Expert plan) trades margin for speed. Sean is doing the opposite: free FTM courthouse data + L1–L4 deep prospecting to compound information asymmetry.
- B's cost-per-contract target ($3K–$5K stacked, $500–$2K FTM-layered) is volume math. Sean's economics are margin math ($25K–$100K+ per niche deal).

### Why not D (Scale-Up)
- D requires 5+ hires and $14K–$24K/mo full-machine spend. Sean's stack tops at $665/mo Full Stack + ~$700/mo Data Manager.
- D's "all 5 channels all the time" with bulk dialers is the opposite of Sniper Marketing.
- D's KPI dashboard is volume-oriented (108 dials/correct #, 50 min dials/caller/day). Sean's KPIs are research-bounded (90-min cap per lead, 22%/38% deceased rates, 20–25% DP-sourced deals).
- D's Phase 11 target is 3–5+ deals/month. Sean's target is 6–12 deals/year at $25K–$100K+ each — same gross, fraction of the headcount and complexity.

### Why C is the right fit
- **FTM operator already** — daily Apify Actor pulling Cuyahoga/Summit/Stark foreclosure + probate, the cheapest, freshest data tier.
- **One Data Manager planned** — matches C's "first and only hire" rule and Quintin/Mara/Brian Johnson model.
- **Niche focus** — OH foreclosure + probate, with state-aware enrichment guarding against TN-leak regressions.
- **Tech budget $0–$665/mo** — sits squarely inside C's Essential ($248–$498) to Full Stack ($288–$665) range.
- **Information asymmetry tooling** — `oh_property_lookup` (Cuyahoga MyPlace + Stark IasWorld), obituary enricher with DOD sanity check, deep-prospecting PDFs to OneDrive — exactly the L2/L3 automation C calls for.
- **Sniper marketing match** — 26 TCA sequences, niche sequential presets (12 niche + 9 bulk, all Sold-excluded), personalized DM-aware contact mapping (PR/executor for deceased, owner for living) is the antithesis of "Dear Homeowner" bulk mail.
- **90-min research cap implementable** — Claude skills (deep-prospecting, probate-property-finder, phone-validator) automate exactly the L2/L3 research C says should be capped at 90 min/lead.

Sean's profile maps to **Phase 6 (Niche Specialist)** of C — 6–12 deals/yr at $25K–$100K+, $500–$2K/mo focused marketing, FTM + skip tracing, heir research and attorney relationships as the moat.
