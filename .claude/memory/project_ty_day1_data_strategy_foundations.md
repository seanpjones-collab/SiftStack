## 1. Data Priority Pyramid

Ty's core principle, verbatim:
> "The harder your first-to-market data is to acquire, the more money you will make."

> "Most investors start at the top of the pyramid instead of the bottom. They jump straight to AI-scored lists before building the courthouse foundation. The result? High spend without a system underneath to convert at scale."

The pyramid is a **sequenced revenue engine**, not a ranking. Each tier funds the next. Never skip tiers.

### Tier 1 — Foundation: First-to-Market Data

> "Free or nearly free data pulled directly from public records before anyone else touches it. This is your highest-converting, lowest-cost tier. The courthouse does not charge you for records that will make you $30,000."

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Monthly Cost | Free – $25 |
| Competing Investors | 1–3 |
| Cost/Contract | $1,000 – $2,000 |
| Freshness | 0–7 days from filing |

**Data Sources:**
- County courthouse records (probate, foreclosure, liens, code violations)
- Tax delinquency lists from the county treasurer
- Building permit records
- Eviction filings from the court clerk
- Absentee owner lists from the assessor

### Tier 2 — Multiplier: Stacked Niche Data

> "Take your Tier 1 revenue and reinvest into the Expert plan ($499/mo) to unlock stacking filters. Layer two or three distress indicators on one property. A probate filing alone is good. A probate filing on a property with code violations and tax delinquency? That is a deal waiting to happen. This tier is where you take first-to-market revenue and multiply it."

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Monthly Cost | $500 (Expert plan $499/mo) |
| Stacking Multiplier | 2–3x |
| Cost/Contract | $3,000 – $5,000 |

**Stacking Examples:**
- Probate + code violation + out-of-state owner
- Tax delinquent + vacant property + high equity
- Pre-foreclosure + divorce filing + long ownership
- Inherited property + failed listing + 10+ years ownership

> "Phil Loesch builds 50-150 hyper-niche lists of 20-50 records each. His first deal from this approach closed in two weeks."

### Tier 3 — Premium: AI and Predictive Data

> "Machine learning models that score every property in a market based on likelihood to sell. The technology is powerful. The key is sequencing: when you layer AI on top of Tiers 1 and 2, it amplifies your edge. Without that foundation, you are competing against every other investor using the same scores."

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Monthly Cost | $1,250+ |
| Competing Investors | 50+ |
| Cost/Contract | $4,000 – $8,000 (without foundation) |

**AI Data Reality:**
- Providers: First American, LexisNexis, Formium
- Backend compute cost: $90,000/month for the provider
- Plan pricing starts at $1,250/month for investors
- Best use: supplement Tier 1 and 2, never replace them

> "AI data is not the problem. Skipping the foundation is."

### Why Sequencing Matters — Ty's Tip

> "I watched an operator spend $4,200 a month on AI lists and close one deal every 90 days. Cost per contract: $12,600. A student in the same market pulled free probate records, skip traced for $0.12 each, and closed in two weeks. Cost per contract: $1,000-2,000. The difference was not data quality. It was the sequence. The student had the foundation built first."

## 2. First-to-Market Data Sourcing

Ty's principle: **"The harder your first-to-market data is to acquire, the more money you will make."** Directly proportional: difficulty = lower competition = higher margins.

### Cost Comparison (verbatim)

| Tier | Cost Per Contract |
|---|---|
| FTM (Tier 1) | $500 – $2,000 |
| AI/Predictive (Tier 3) | $4,000 – $8,000 (without FTM foundation) |

### The Six FTM List Types

| Type | Difficulty | Where | Format | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Probate** | Hard | Probate Court Clerk, Surrogate's Court (NY/NJ), Register of Wills (PA/MD), County Clerk Probate Section | Case filings with PR number, decedent name, executor info. Often no property address | Free pull. Skip tracing $0.10–0.15/record. Deep prospecting ~$1–4/record with Claude |
| **Tax Sale / Tax-Delinquent** | Medium | County Treasurer, Tax Collector's Office, Trustee's Office (TN), Commissioner of Revenue | PDF schedules, Excel downloads, online portals | Free. Some counties charge nominal fee for bulk |
| **Foreclosure** | Medium | County Clerk Civil Division, Register of Deeds, newspaper legal notices | Online docket searches, newspaper publication lists | Free. **38% deceased owner rate** means deep prospecting often needed |
| **Pre-Foreclosure** | Medium | County Recorder, Register of Deeds, court docket | Notice of default filings | Free. Some subscription portals $25–50/mo |
| **Code Violations** | Easy | City Code Enforcement, Building & Zoning, Community Development | Online portals, FOIA exports | Free. FOIA fees $5–25 |
| **Eviction Records** | Medium | General Sessions Court, Civil Court Clerk, Small Claims | Court docket searches | Free. Skip trace landlord, not tenant |

### Probate Specifics
- **22-38% of probate records have deceased owners needing deep prospecting**
- Case type search: Estate, Probate, Administration, Heirship Determination
- Date range: Last 30-90 days for fresh cases
- Key data: Decedent name, case/PR number, executor name and address, date of death
- **Missing Address Problem:** Filings give case # + decedent name + executor info; usually NO property address
- Manual lookup: 20-45 min per record at $1-3 cost, 10-15/day at variable accuracy
- Probate Property Finder skill: 2-5 min per record, 50-100/day, high accuracy

### Foreclosure Specifics
- **Median time from notice to auction: 34 days in Knox County**
- Marketing window: Day 1-30
- Lis Pendens timing: Day 1, Digital, Full data, Low competition
- Newspaper Notice timing: Day 20+, Text format, Partial data, Higher competition

### Sourcing Strategies (3 paths)

1. **Online County Portals** — easiest path. Search "[County Name] + [record type] + search"
2. **In-Person Courthouse Pulls** — bring photo ID, phone/camera, USB drive, list of record types. One trip covers 2-3 list types
3. **Publication Notices** — newspaper legal notice sections, county-designated legal pub sites, state aggregators, county "Public Notices" page

### Difficulty Spectrum

| Tier | Description | Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| **Easy** | Online, exportable, free | Recurring weekly/monthly calendar reminder. Tax delinquency Excel, code violation portals |
| **Medium** | Online but limited (CAPTCHA, single-search, subscription) | FOIA requests; bulk data $50–200/year |
| **Hard** | In-person only | TaskRabbit ($20/hr) or Investor Bootz; one trip covers 2-3 list types |
| **Needs Help** | Special access required (affidavits, license verification) | Local courthouse runner; ongoing relationship |

### TaskRabbit Economics
- **TaskRabbit ~$20/hr** under "Personal Assistant" category
- **One Trip ROI: $40-80** covers 2-3 list types per visit
- > "If one lead from that trip closes, the ROI is 100x or more."

### The 6-Step FTM Pipeline
1. **County Source**
2. **Extract** — Download, photograph, or FOIA
3. **Clean & Format** — Remove non-residential, verify types
4. **Skip Trace** — DataSift ($0.10-0.15/rec) → Skip Genie → Forewarn (if licensed)
5. **Upload to CRM** — Tag by list type, assign filters
6. **Niche Sequential** — 3-day cadence: Call, Mail, Text

### Tax Sale PDF Extraction Rule
> "Use Claude to extract structured data from PDF tax sale lists." Prompt: "Extract this tax sale data into a CSV with columns: Property Address, Owner Name, Amount Owed, Auction Date, Parcel ID."

Filter for residential properties with **2+ years of delinquency**. High equity = strong motivation indicator.

### FTM Tax Sale Variants
- **Auction Schedule Lists** — published 30-60 days before auction
- **Tax Delinquency Lists** — full unpaid list (often thousands), annual publication
- **Tax Lien Certificates** — focus on certificates approaching redemption deadline

### Probate Processing for CRM
- **Owner swap:** Change CRM record from deceased to executor/heir; update mailing address
- **Tag by situation:** "Probate" + sub-tags (multiple heirs, vacant, occupied by family)
- **Deep prospecting queue:** 22-38% need L3-L4 research
- **Not-interested cadence:** **Probate 45-day** vs Foreclosure 15-day vs General 90-day

### FTM County Data Skill — 3 Priority Tiers

| Priority | Lists |
|---|---|
| **A — Core** | Probate/Heirship, Foreclosure/Auction, Tax Sale, Tax Delinquency |
| **B — Standard** | Code Violations, Condemned Structures, Mechanic's Liens, IRS/State Tax Liens |
| **C — Extended** | HOA/Condo Liens, Utility Shut-offs, Building Permits (demolition/repair), Bankruptcy filings |

## 3. Market Finder Workflow

> "Most investors pick a market, then try to make it work. That is backwards. The Market Finder inverts the process."

> "I'd rather be the person at 4K across seven different counties... scale right, but do less people that are more targeted in more counties."

### The 4 Key Metrics (used at every level)

1. **Median Sales Price** — what deals close at. Look for: sales price below median home value
2. **Days on Market** — speed of absorption. **Red flag: DOM above county average.** Knox County's 37902 (downtown) shows 112 DOM despite being on the heat map
3. **Median Home Value** — price range anchor. Compare against **$375K national average**
4. **Investor Transaction Volume** — proof of market viability. "Pick areas with solid volume, not the absolute highest"

### The 60% Price Range Rule
> "Stay within the middle 60% of home values in your market. Minnesota example: $200K-$600K. The edges attract either institutional buyers (high end) or properties with deferred maintenance disasters (low end). The middle is where individual investors make money."

### Market Finder FAQ (verbatim)
- **Investor transaction definition:** "A sale where an LLC or company purchased the property from an individual. LLCs or Trusts selling to another company or trust are excluded from the count."
- **Recency:** "Homes Sold reflects the last completed month. Investor Transactions use the last six completed months."
- **Best single metric:** "There is no single best metric. Use DOM + Months of Inventory + Investor Transactions + Price fit together."

### The 4-Phase Progressive Drill-Down

**Phase 1 — State-Level**
- Compare median home value against **$375K national average**. Prefer states at or under it
- Check DOM vs national average. Lower is better
- Demand: homes on market vs homes sold. **Want less than 3 months of supply**
- Identify the **60% price range**

**Phase 2 — County-Level**
- Same 4 metrics
- **Ideal county: median sales price below median home value, DOM below county average, healthy demand ratio**
- Rank top 3

**Phase 3 — Zip Code Analysis**
- Switch to zip code view
- Investor transaction heat map
- **Do not just pick the darkest zip code.** High volume with high DOM is a trap
- Select **top 3-5 zips** that pass all four checks within 60% price range

**Phase 4 — Neighborhood Analysis**
- Select **top 3-8 neighborhoods**
- "Bad zip codes can contain great neighborhoods. Good zip codes can have dead pockets."
- Unlocks hyper-targeted strategies: direct mail, door knocking, FB retargeting, D4D

### Months of Supply Thresholds
- **Under 3 months** = seller's market (prices rising, tough negotiation)
- **3–6 months** = balanced (Knox County 3.9 sits here)
- **Over 6 months** = buyer's market (more leverage, slower sales)

## 5. Sold Properties Analysis

> "Sold Properties shows what actually sold in the last 6 months. Not what should sell. Not what you hope will sell. What did sell, to whom, and at what price."

> "The distressors on properties at time of sale tell you what lists to pull. Not the other way around. Most investors pick distressors first and hope the data confirms their choice. Flip that. Let the sold data choose your lists."

**Access requirement:** SiftMap Pro ($297/mo standalone, included in Expert $499/mo and AI $1,250/mo).

### The Validation Loop (4 Steps, Quarterly)

1. **Assume** — Set base criteria in SiftMap from Market Finder data
2. **Verify** — Open Sold Properties, last 6 months, Investor Transactions tab, AI 50-100
3. **Correlate** — Which distressors? At what price points? Which zips? Click individual transactions for buyers + repeat purchasers
4. **Refine** — Adjust filters, build refined presets, run loop again next quarter

### Three Tabs

| Tab | What It Answers |
|---|---|
| **All Transactions** | Total market velocity baseline |
| **Investor Transactions** | LLC/company purchases from individuals (excludes LLC-to-LLC, manufactured homes, double closings). The primary analysis tab |
| **In My Records** | CRM properties that actually sold. Accountability check |

### Top Patterns — 5 Dimensions
1. Price Range
2. Property Type
