## 2. Company Audit

### Framework: 4-step diagnostic

1. **Track Expenses** — monthly revenue, tools/marketing, overhead, team
2. **Score Yourself** — 20 questions × 4 pillars (Marketing / Sales Mgmt / Operations / KPIs), 1-5 scale
3. **Review Dashboard** — overall score, pillar breakdown, radar chart, bottleneck ranking
4. **Read Analysis** — every question scored with status, action items

### Score Legend (verbatim)

- **UGLY:** 5-10 (out of 25) per pillar / 20-40 overall
- **BAD:** 11-15 / 41-60
- **GOOD:** 16-20 / 61-80
- **NINJA:** 21-25 / 81-100

> "Score yourself honestly. Not where you want to be. Not where you think you should be. Where you actually are today. The only person you hurt by inflating scores is yourself."

### The 20 Questions (exact text)

**Marketing (5 × 1-5 = /25):**
1. We have a systematic process to acquire and market new data.
2. It is easy to track what data is being marketed and strategies used.
3. We use multiple marketing channels without duplicate targeting.
4. We consistently generate leads for the specific problems we solve.
5. We have a process to reach owners who ignored previous marketing.

**Sales Management (5 × 1-5 = /25):**
1. New leads are followed up and qualified quickly.
2. Tasks are completed on time based on lead temperature.
3. We have a repeatable handoff process from Lead Mgmt → Acquisitions → Transactions → Dispositions.
4. All offers and seller communication are documented in the CRM.
5. Leads and deals are routinely audited for lost opportunity.

**Operations (5 × 1-5 = /25):**
1. What is your net profit margin? (1=0%, 2=1-20%, 3=21-40%, 4=41-50%, 5=above 50%)
2. All employees execute in alignment with company mission, vision, values.
3. All team members know their roles and daily/weekly/monthly tasks.
4. Team members know where SOPs are and use them consistently.
5. Flowcharts and process documentation are updated routinely.

**KPIs (5 × 1-5 = /25):**
1. KPIs are used to measure and monitor progress across the business.
2. KPIs are reviewed regularly, broken down by role.
3. KPI data is used to adjust strategies and maximize results.
4. There is a process to diagnose and fix nonperforming KPIs.
5. All team actions are tracked and logged daily.

### Audit cadence

- **Re-audit every 90 days** (quarterly).
- The cash conversion cycle in REI is 90+ days, so quarterly aligns with first-revenue from new marketing.
- Fix **only one bottleneck (lowest-scoring pillar) at a time**.

### The Quarterly Growth Engine (5-stage cycle)

1. **AUDIT** — score 4 pillars
2. **PLAN** — set 30/60/90 (3-7 quarterly rocks)
3. **EXECUTE** — 9-5 income-producing, after-5 improvement
4. **REVIEW** — what worked, what stalled, why
5. **RE-AUDIT** — compare to last quarter, set next rocks

### Business Type Benchmarks

| | Lifestyle | Growth |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue | $200K-$500K/yr | $500K-$5M+/yr |
| Net Margin | 50%+ | 20% min |
| Team Size | 1-2 (solo or one assistant) | 5+ (full acquisition team) |

### Hard rules

- "Nothing downstream matters without strong lead generation. If Marketing scored lowest, stop everything else and fix your data and channel systems first."
- "If you cannot sustain your current overhead for 6 months without a deal, your first bottleneck is cash flow, not marketing."
- "Expect 90+ days from marketing start to first closed revenue."
- 30/60/90 plan: Days 1-30 fix lowest pillar / 31-60 strengthen second-lowest / 61-90 build systems for third.

## 4. KPI Tracking

### Reverse-engineer revenue (3 inputs, 6 outputs)

Inputs: monthly revenue goal, average deal profit, close rate %.
Outputs: deals/mo, offers/mo, appointments/mo, conversations/mo, dials/mo, contracts signed/wk.

> "If your daily dial number looks scary, you need more callers, not more motivation. One caller can do 150-200 dials/day with a power dialer. Divide your monthly dial target by 150 to see how many callers you need."

### The funnel ratios (memorize)

- **Dials → Correct Numbers: 32:1** (Trestle scoring can drop to 8-10:1)
- **Correct Numbers / Conversations → Appointments: 5:1**
- **Appointments → Offers Made: 1.5:1** (offer on ~2 of every 3)
- **Offers → Contracts Signed: 4:1** (1 in 4 accepted is solid)

### Per-role KPI targets

**Cold Caller / Prospector:**
- Dials/day: **150-200**
- Conversations/day: **5-8**
- Appointments set/day: **3-5**
- Weekly dial minimum: **5,500+**
- Hours on dialer/day: **4-6**
- Cost per lead: track weekly
- **The one metric: Appointments set per dial hour.** Caller doing 200 dials w/ 1 appt is less valuable than one doing 120 dials w/ 4.

**Lead Manager:**
- Follow-up attempts/day: **50**
- Offers submitted/day: **3-5**
- All tasks cleared: **daily**
- Speed to lead (new inbound): **<1 minute**
- Pipeline review: weekly
- Hot/Warm/Cold counts: weekly report
- **The one metric: Lead-to-offer conversion.** Speed to lead matters: **400% higher conversion when responding within 1 minute.**

**Closer / Acquisitions:**
- Offers/day: **5-10**
- Hot leads contacted within 1 hour
- Contracts/week: track
- Offer-to-contract rate: **25%+ target**
- Projected profit/deal: per deal
- Days to close: track avg
- **The one metric: Offer-to-contract ratio.** If <20%, comps are off or offers too aggressive.

**Data Manager:**
- Records/day: **200-500**
- <5% duplicate rate
- Daily FTM uploads, weekly niche

### KPI Sheet (Google Sheets, 10 tabs)

1. **Start Here** — company info, instructions
2. **Goals & Setup** — revenue targets, avg deal size, close rate
3. **Expenses** — monthly cost tracking
4. **Monthly Data** — aggregate monthly numbers
5. **Dashboard** — visual KPI summary (green/red on-pace)
6. **Review Dashboard** — month-over-month trends, conversion rates, cost per lead over time
7. **Cold Caller / Prospector Tracker** — daily per-caller. **Blue columns admin-only (from dialer), yellow columns caller self-updates. Separation prevents gaming.** Weekly target: **5,500+ dials minimum per caller.**
8. **Lead Manager Tracker** — weekly: inbound, outbound follow-ups, warm/hot moves, appointments, offers
9. **Transactions** — deal-by-deal: address, purchase, rehab, ARV, projected vs actual profit
10. **Direct Mail** — per-campaign: list name, pieces mailed, date, cost, responses, cost per response. Mail = third touch in sequential ($0.50-$2.00/piece).

### Tally Counter Method

Free Chrome extension at tallycount.app. Counters for: Dials, Correct Numbers, Conversations, Appointments. **Zero-friction tracking at point of action.** Transfer to KPI Sheet end of day, reset.

> "Ask callers to alt-tab to a spreadsheet after every call. They will stop doing it by day 3 and your data goes dark."

### Manual Sheet vs Platform Dashboards (use both)

- **Google Sheet:** strategic planning, goal-setting, team reviews, long-term trends. **Manual entry forces accountability — you can't type "47 dials" without knowing it should have been 150.**
- **DataSift dashboards:** real-time pulse check during workday, refreshed every 5 min, zero manual entry.

> "When a number auto-populates on a dashboard, you glance at it. When you manually type that number into a spreadsheet, you feel it."

### Review cadence

- **Daily (30 min):** check yesterday's dials vs target, review Tally numbers, check LM follow-up completion, review overnight leads (speed to lead), clear CRM task queue (STABM), update KPI Sheet daily columns.
- **Weekly (1 hr):** team review on conversion by role, update Monthly Data with weekly rollup, check cost per lead trend, pipeline review hot/warm/cold.
- **Monthly (2 hrs):** full Dashboard review, cost per contract, team review + goal adjustment.

For teams of 3+: daily Basecamp / Slack auto-check-ins asking "Did you post your KPIs?". Optional: DataBox ($50+/mo) for real-time dashboards.

## 5. SOP Creation

### The Hire-Without-Training Trap

> "You hire a VA. You spend two weeks training them live, on calls, over Zoom. They get decent at the job. Then they leave after 60 days. Start over. From scratch. Because the knowledge lived in your head, not a system."

**SOP-First Rule:** never hire before processes are documented. Build SOPs **before posting job listings**.

### From 40-min ramble → 11-page playbook

The Playbook Creator skill takes raw transcripts (typically 40 min, 6,200 words, zero structure) and produces:
- 4-Phase Process Map with decision gates
- 6 Numbered Steps (Goal / Actions / Decision Gates / Screenshot Placeholders / Pro Tips)
- 2 Decision Trees (e.g., property type routing, verification)
- Worked Example with real address data
- Quality Checklist (6 verification items)

### The 5-Step SOP Pipeline

1. **LEARN the Skill** — do the task yourself first (1-4 hrs first attempt). Cannot document what you have not done.
2. **RECORD it** — Loom (or Fireflies for meetings). 20-60 min per recording. **Narrate decisions, not just clicks.**
3. **TRANSCRIBE** — Loom auto-transcribes; copy full text. 2 min. **Don't edit before feeding to skill — give it everything.**
4. **FEED to Playbook Creator** — paste full transcript. Skill extracts maps, trees, steps, screenshot placeholders. 3-5 min.
5. **POLISH + DEPLOY** — review output, replace screenshot placeholders with actual screenshots, store in Google Drive by role. 15-30 min.

### Recording tools

- **Loom** — primary. Free tier. Screen + camera, auto-transcription. Solo screen-based tasks.
- **Fireflies.ai** — meetings (Google Meet / Zoom). Bot participant. Live training calls + Q&A.
- **Live training + Teach Claude (Chrome ext)** — in-person / phone-based / browser-heavy.

### What the skill produces

- SOP / Playbook document
- Mermaid process maps (**max 7 nodes per diagram** — verbatim from skill spec)
- Decision trees for every fork
- Screenshot placeholders
- **5th-grade reading level** (target readability — short sentences, simple vocab, no undefined jargon)
- Pro tips extracted from your narration

**Cost:** Claude Pro at $20+/mo. **No Max plan required.**

- Source: 40-min training call
- Output: 11 pages, 6 steps, 2 decision trees, 7 pro tips, 1 worked example (All Tassel Pike, Knox County)
- Phases: Research → Access → Extract → Upload
- Decision trees: "Is This a Real Foreclosure?" (Lis Pendens check) + "Property Type Routing" (residential/commercial/land)
- Step format: Goal / Actions / Decision Gates / Screenshot Placeholders / Pro Tips
- Quality checklist: data formatting correct, all fields populated, dups removed, status verified, CRM upload confirmed, screenshot of final list saved

### Best practices (do/don't)

**Do:** narrate decisions not just clicks ("I check Zillow here because the county site doesn't show renovation status"). Include edge cases ("If mobile home, skip — can't finance"). Feed multiple recordings of same process for richer output.

**Don't:** rush the recording. Skip the screenshot step (cuts training questions by half). Treat first output as final — review and walk through yourself first.

> "Score yourself honestly. Not where you want to be. Not where you think you should be. Where you actually are today. The only person you hurt by inflating scores is yourself."

> "Nothing downstream matters without strong lead generation. If Marketing scored lowest, stop everything else and fix your data and channel systems first."

> "If you cannot sustain your current overhead for 6 months without a deal, your first bottleneck is cash flow, not marketing."

> "Hiring is not about ambition. It is about math. Every hour you spend on a $500/month task is an hour you are not spending on a $5,000 activity."

> "Budget for 6 months of your new hire's salary without counting on revenue. Hiring on hope is the fastest way to kill a business."

> "Pay on time, every time. International VAs talk to each other. One late payment and your reputation in the community is done."

> "An underperforming team member costs you 2-3 deals per month in opportunity cost. The fear of firing costs more than the inconvenience of rehiring."

> "If someone cannot record a Loom, they cannot do the job."

**KPI philosophy:**

> "80% of REI businesses that stall had enough leads. They had a tracking problem, not a lead problem."

> "KPIs do not make you a micromanager. They make you a leader who can diagnose problems in 30 seconds instead of 30 days."

> "Tracking changes behavior even before you act on the data. When a caller knows their numbers are visible, their dial count goes up 15-20% without you saying a word. Visibility is accountability."

> "When a number auto-populates on a dashboard, you glance at it. When you manually type that number into a spreadsheet, you feel it. That constant feel for your numbers is the accountability layer no automation can replicate. The operators who scale fastest are the ones who still hand-enter their KPIs even after they have dashboards that could do it for them."

> "If your daily dial number looks scary, you need more callers, not more motivation."

> "Ask callers to alt-tab to a spreadsheet after every call. They will stop doing it by day 3 and your data goes dark."

**SOP philosophy:**

> "You hire a VA. You spend two weeks training them live, on calls, over Zoom, answering the same questions repeatedly. They get decent at the job. Then they leave after 60 days. Start over. From scratch. Because the knowledge lived in your head, not a system. That is not a hiring problem. That is a documentation problem."

> "I actually use the Playbook Creator internally at DataSift too. Every new process gets recorded, transcribed, and fed through the skill before anyone else touches it." — Ty

> "Your SOP library is your company's most valuable asset after your deal pipeline. When a VA leaves, you lose a person. When you lose your SOPs, you lose the system."

> "Build SOPs before posting job listings. Your first hire should read the system on Day 1, not wait for you to be available."

> "The more thorough and detailed you are, the better you will get the outputs back."

**Execution rhythm:**

> "9-5 is income-producing activities: calling, following up, closing. After 5 is improvement projects: building SOPs, setting up dashboards, refining processes. Both matter. Do not sacrifice income for infrastructure."

> "Even Ty and Tyler are not 5s across the board. The audit identifies your single biggest bottleneck so you stop guessing and start fixing."
