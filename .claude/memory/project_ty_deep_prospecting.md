# Deep Prospecting (Ty's Official DataSift Curriculum)

Source: `tmp/datasift_learn/deep_prospecting.md`

## Why Deep Prospecting Is Your Highest-ROI Skill

Sequential marketing handles the easy leads. The other 22-38% of the list — dead owners, wrong addresses, three generations of heirs with no probate filed — are where deep prospecting begins. "Not as a replacement for sequential marketing, but as the next step when it fails. The harder it is to reach someone, the less competition you have and the bigger the deals."

Headline numbers:
- **$1.50–$4** cost per touch (deep prospecting)
- **22–38%** deceased owners on lists
- **0** competing investors
- **~90%** wrong numbers from skip traces — "Restricted marketing skip traces return roughly 10 possible phone numbers per record. Only one is correct. That means ~90% wrong number rates are normal. When your skip trace 'fails,' it does not mean bad data. It means you need to verify which number is real. That is what deep prospecting does."

## Four Levels of Deep Prospecting

| Level | Name | Tools | Cost | Time |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| L1 | Enhanced Skip Tracing | DataSift Skip Trace, Skip Genie, Forewarn | $0.15/record – $97/mo | Bulk processing |
| L2 | Ownership Verification | County Recorder, Google | Free–$25 | 15–30 min/record |
| L3 | Deceased Owner / Heir Research | FindAGrave, Ancestry | $25–50/mo | 1–3 hrs/record |
| L4 | Curative Title Work | Title Attorney, PACER | $500–$2,000+ | Days to weeks |

### L1: Enhanced Skip Tracing
Run records through multiple skip trace platforms in sequence. Start with DataSift's built-in skip trace, then push remaining records through Skip Genie for a second pass. If you have a real estate license, Forewarn has the best data available.

### L2: Ownership Verification
Public records show conflicting names, addresses, or ownership history. Pull the deed chain from the county recorder. Look for middle initials, transaction types, and installment agreements. These clues reveal whether the recorded owner is the actual decision-maker.

### L3: Deceased Owner / Heir Research
Owner is dead. No probate filed. You need to find the heirs, verify who is alive, and locate their current contact information. "Go back to go forward" is the principle: research older records and deceased relatives to find paths to living heirs.

### L4: Curative Title Work
Multiple generations of heirs. Partial ownership disputes. Clouded title requiring court action. Often requires a title attorney. "The deals here can be massive because virtually no one else has the patience or skill to close them."

## Three Phases: Verify, Research, Locate

Every deep prospect follows the same three-phase workflow. **Critical fork: if the owner is alive, skip Phase 2 entirely.**

- **PHASE 1 — Verify & Review:** Pull deed, check records. Confirm ownership from deed. Check middle initials, dates. Look for "estate of" flag.
- **PHASE 2 — Research & Map** (only if owner is dead): Obituaries, heirs, family tree. FindAGrave: confirm death. Ancestry: build family tree. "Go back to go forward."
- **PHASE 3 — Locate & Contact:** 3-site skip trace waterfall. TruePeopleSearch first. FastPeopleSearch second. CyberBackgroundChecks third.

### The 3-Site Skip Trace Waterfall
"Once you identify the decision-maker (living owner or heir), run their name through TruePeopleSearch, then FastPeopleSearch, then CyberBackgroundChecks. If the same phone number appears on 2 or more sites, you have a high-confidence match. All three are free."

## Deep Prospecting Tools, Ranked

### Phase 1: Verify
- **County Deed Records** — Free–$25. Pull the deed chain. Look for ownership transfer dates, middle initials, "estate of" flags, and installment agreements.
- **Google Dorking** — Free. Advanced search operators. Use `site:findagrave.com` + owner name for quick death verification.
- **BeenVerified** — $27–$37/mo. Primary skip tracing tool for deep prospecting. "Frame your search as 'title work' for best results. $1 trial available."

### Phase 2: Research
- **FindAGrave.com** — Free. Confirm death records, discover cemetery location, find family member connections in memorial entries. **First stop for death verification.**
- **Ancestry.com** — $24.99–$49.99/mo. Build family trees, find obituary full text, trace heirs across generations. "The U.S. Discovery plan at $24.99/mo is sufficient for heir research."
- **Newspapers.com** — Included with Ancestry. Search historical obituaries and public notices.

### Phase 3: Locate
- **TruePeopleSearch** — Free. First stop in the waterfall. Check "Also Known As" names for maiden names and aliases. Cross-reference relatives listed.
- **FastPeopleSearch** — Free. Second in the waterfall. Cross-reference TruePeopleSearch.
- **CyberBackgroundChecks** — Free. Third in the waterfall. Check "Relatives/Associates" section for additional leads. **Phone on 2+ sites equals high confidence.**

## The Heir Verification Loop (6 Steps)

1. **Confirm Death** — Search FindAGrave.com and Google for the owner name + "obituary." Verify death date matches deed inactivity timeline.
2. **Read the Obituary** — Extract every name mentioned: spouse, children, grandchildren, siblings. Note "survived by" vs "preceded in death by" language carefully.
3. **Build the Family Tree** — Use Ancestry.com to map all heirs. Look for "Sr." naming conventions (proves at least one child exists). Cross-reference maiden names from marriage records.
4. **Mark Living vs. Deceased** — For each heir, verify alive or dead. Cross-reference FindAGrave, Social Security Death Index, and Facebook. Dead heirs create more branches to trace.
5. **Identify the Decision-Maker** — Find the surviving spouse (right of survivorship) or the heir most likely to control the property. Prioritize the one with the simplest chain of title.
6. **Run the 3-Site Skip Trace Waterfall** — Phone confirmed on 2+ sites = high confidence. Update CRM with the heir name and new mailing address.

### Do
- Start with FindAGrave before paying for Ancestry
- Document every heir in your CRM with status (alive/deceased/unknown)
- Use the "Owner Swap" in DataSift to update to the heir's name and address
- Process returned mail records first (highest ROI deep prospects)

### Don't
- Skip the verification loop and cold-call every number from a skip trace
- Assume one skip trace provider gives you everything
- Ignore "estate of" designations in county records
- Deep prospect records that still have working phone numbers (use sequential marketing first)

## Deep Prospecting Board in DataSift

- **Phone Status filter:** include only Wrong DNC, Wrong, and Dead numbers.
- **Call Attempts filter:** set min/max thresholds (e.g., 10+ attempts with no contact).

Statuses → Create Custom Status → label "Deep Prospecting" → pick a color → save.

2. Call New Numbers

## Key Terms (Verbatim)

- **L1: Enhanced Skip Tracing** — Multiple skip trace platforms in sequence: DataSift first, then Skip Genie, then Forewarn (if licensed).
- **L2: Ownership Verification** — Pulling deed chain from county records to verify actual owner.
- **L3: Deceased Owner** — Owner dead, no probate. Trace heirs through obituaries, family trees, genealogy.
- **L4: Curative Title** — Clouded title with multiple heirs, partial ownership, or court proceedings. Usually needs a title attorney.
- **3-Site Skip Trace Waterfall** — TruePeopleSearch → FastPeopleSearch → CyberBackgroundChecks. Same phone on 2+ sites = high confidence. All free.
- **Owner Swap** — After identifying correct heir/executor, update Owner Name and Mailing Address in DataSift.
- **"Estate Of" Designation** — County records showing "Estate of [Name]" confirm deceased owner. "Lowest-hanging fruit for deep prospecting."
- **"Go Back to Go Forward"** — Core genealogy principle: research older records, deceased relatives, and historical documents to discover paths to living heirs.
