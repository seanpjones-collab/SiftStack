## The 980,000-Mailer Validation

"I pulled the last six months of mailer data from every user on the DataSift platform. **980,000 mailers total.** I broke down which campaigns drove the most revenue, which mailer types earned the highest response rates, and which structures outperformed. **One pattern stood out above everything else: operators who rotated mailer types monthly crushed those who sent the same template every 30 days.** Everything I teach about winning mailer strategy on this page comes from that internal dataset."

Direct mail sits third in the sequential marketing order. After SMS ($0.01/touch) and cold calling ($0.03-$0.06/touch), mail at $0.50-$2.00/touch is the first physical channel.

## The Three Mailers

| Mailer | Price (Business+) | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Real Penned Letter | $1.79/piece | Niche Sequential Day 1 |
| Snapback Check | $0.67–$1.90/piece | Re-engagement & Follow-Up |
| Family/Local Postcard | $0.60–$0.77/piece | Bulk Sequential & Volume |

### Real Penned Handwritten Letter
A6 envelope in Lavender or Navy Blue. Real ink (machine mimics human handwriting). Forever stamp = 1-3 day delivery (vs 5-7 for presorted bulk). **No tracking barcode on envelope** — looks like real letter from a real person.

- **When:** Day 1 of niche sequential. Freshest first-to-market leads (probate, tax sale, foreclosure). Small lists of 200-500 records.
- **Why:** Forever stamp delivers in 1-3 days while you're calling Days 1-3.
- **Cost math:** 300 records × $1.79 = $537/month. One deal covers that for a year.
- **No tracking:** "DataSift cannot track delivery because forever stamps bypass USPS tracking systems."

### Snapback Check
Looks like a real check with a soft offer amount. Built using 6×11 postcard ($0.67) or 6×18 bi-fold self-mailer ($1.90).

- **When:** Not-interested follow-ups (quarterly rotation). Warm/hot lead re-engagement. Leads who answered phone but said no.
- **Why:** "Someone who said 'not interested' three months ago sees a specific dollar amount. That changes the conversation from 'do you want to sell' to 'is this number close?'"
- **Rotation:** "Alternate with postcards monthly. Never send the same check design two cycles in a row."

### Family/Local Postcard
4×6, 6×9, or 6×11 with family photo, local landmarks, community branding. Starts at $0.60/piece on Business plan. **Cheapest mailer.**

- **When:** Bulk sequential (5K+ records). Monthly rehash of exhausted phone lists. Long-touch follow-up for aged data.
- **Cost math:** "At $0.60/piece, you can mail 5,000 records for $3,000/month. That same budget buys 120 door knocks at $25 each."
- **Street View add-on:** +$0.02/mailer adds property's Google Street View. "Worth it for lists under 2,000."
- **Size strategy:** 4×6 for high-volume bulk (cheapest). 6×11 for targeted campaigns.

## When to Use Each Mailer

### Niche Sequential (FTM, <1K records)
- **Day 1:** Call all numbers (attempt 1) + trigger handwritten letter (forever stamp).
- **Day 2:** Call (attempt 2) + voicemail + text.
- **Day 3:** Call (attempt 3) + voicemail + text.
- **After 3 attempts exhausted:** Move to monthly mailer rotation. Alternate between postcard and snapback check each month. **Never the same mailer two months in a row.**

### Bulk Sequential (5K+ records)
- **Phase 1: Dial** — Power dialer (ReadyMode, Smarter Contact). 6-8 call attempts.
- **Phase 2: Mail** — Family/local postcard at $0.60/piece. Alternate with snapback check monthly.
- **Phase 3: Deep Prospecting** — Exhausted, unreachable. $1.50-$4.00/touch.

**Monthly rotation rule:** "Postcard month 1, snapback check month 2, postcard month 3. Six months of platform-wide send data (980,000 mailers) confirms rotation outperforms repetition across every list size and market."

### Not-Interested Follow-Up Cadences
"20-30% of all platform deals come from leads who initially said no. Rotate your mailer type each cycle."

#### Do
- **Probate: mail every 45 days**
- **Foreclosure: mail every 15 days**
- **General not-interested: mail every 90 days (quarterly)**
- Rotate mailer each cycle (postcard, then check, then letter)
- Exclude returned and undeliverable records

#### Don't
- Send the same mailer two months in a row
- Mail to vacant addresses (waste of credits)
- Skip the "Do Not Mail" filter (legal risk)
- Treat all not-interested leads the same (cadence varies by property type)
- Mail without calling first (mail is the 3rd channel, not the 1st)

## Setting Up Direct Mail
1. Open Direct Mail Settings (gear icon top-right).
2. Enter Default Return Address (where returned mail goes — business mailing address or PO Box).
3. Enter Default Sender Contact (name, phone, email — populates Sender Fields).

## Designing Mailers (Template Editor)
1. Choose Mailer Type — postcards (3 sizes), personal letters, professional letters, real penned letters, or self-mailers.
2. Start From Scratch or Use a Template.
3. Add Variable Fields — Contact Fields (recipient data) + Sender Fields (your info).
4. Add QR Code & Street View — QR codes free; Street View +$0.02/piece (renders at print, not preview).
5. Use AI Write for Copy — rewrite, shorten, continue, proofread, rephrase.

"Create at least three template variations per mailer type before launching your first campaign. You need them for the monthly rotation. A handwritten letter template, two postcard designs (family photo and local landmarks), and a snapback check layout gives you a four-month rotation before repeating."

## 8 Direct Mail Filter Presets

### Generating Leads (4)

#### 1. Skip Traced, No Numbers (Both Flows)
**Who:** Records skip traced but returned zero phone numbers.
**Filters:** Property Status: exclude all. DM Attempts: 0. Skip Traced: Yes. Numbers: No. Vacant Mailing: No. Last Mail Status: exclude Returned and Undeliverable.
**Frequency:** Weekly or monthly.

#### 2. No Contact After Marketing (Bulk Sequential)
**Who:** Called 5+ times, texted 3+ times, no response. "Bulk sequential handoff: power dialer did its job, now mail takes over."
**Filters:** Property Status: exclude all. DM Attempts: 0. Call Attempts: min 5. SMS Attempts: min 3. Vacant Mailing: No. Last Mail Status: exclude Returned and Undeliverable.

#### 3. All Numbers Bad (Wrong/Dead/DNC) (Both Flows)
**Who:** Every phone wrong, dead, or DNC.
**Filters:** Phone Status Combination: all Wrong, Dead, DNC. Property Status: exclude all. DM Attempts: 0. Vacant Mailing: No. Last Mail Status: exclude Returned and Undeliverable.

#### 4. Monthly Mail Rehash (Bulk Sequential)
**Who:** Already mailed 1-12 times, last mail >1 month ago.
**Filters:** Property Status: exclude all. DM Attempts: min 1, max 6 or 12. Last Direct Mail Date: Prior to, more than 1 month ago. Vacant Mailing: No. Last Mail Status: exclude Returned and Undeliverable.
**Frequency:** Monthly. **"This is where the mailer rotation rule applies: different template each month."**

### Following Up (4)

#### 5. Leads Not Mailed (Both Flows)
**Who:** Lead status (New, Cold, Warm, Hot, No Contact New) never mailed.
**Filters:** Property Status: include New Lead, Cold Lead, Warm Lead, Hot Lead, No Contact New Lead. DM Attempts: 0/0. Vacant Mailing: No.

#### 6. Leads Mail Monthly (Both Flows)
**Who:** Active leads last mailed >1 month ago.
**Filters:** Property Status: include lead statuses. Last Direct Mail Date: Prior to, more than 1 month ago. Last Mail Status: exclude Returned and Undeliverable. Vacant Mailing: No.

#### 7. Not Interested, Not Mailed (Both Flows)
**Who:** Said no on phone, never mailed. **"This is where the snapback check shines."**
**Filters:** Property Status: include Not Interested. DM Attempts: 0/0. Vacant Mailing: No.

#### 8. Not Interested, Mail Quarterly (Both Flows)
**Who:** Not-interested, last mailed >1 quarter ago. "**The 20-30% deal machine.** Quarterly not-interested follow-up. Rotate between snapback check and family postcard each quarter."
**Filters:** Property Status: include Not Interested. Last Direct Mail Date: Prior to, more than 1 quarter ago. DM Attempts: optional, min 1, max 6-12. Vacant Mailing: No.

**Ty's batching rhythm:** "I batch presets into two weekly sessions. Mondays I run presets 1-3 (generating leads) to catch new mail-only records from last week's skip traces. Fridays I run presets 5-8 (follow-ups) to queue lead nurture mail for the next week. Preset 4 (monthly rehash) runs on the first Monday of each month with a different template than the previous month."

**Use "Prior to" dates not "Fixed":** "'Prior to last month' or 'Prior to last quarter' dynamically recalculate every time you load the preset."

## Mail Status Tracking (7 Statuses)

- **Scheduled** — Pending order processing
- **Processing** — Printing phase, sent 2 days before mail date
- **En Route** — Printed and dropped at USPS
- **Delivered** — Local USPS received mail
- **Undeliverable** — Address invalid. **Credits refunded. Never printed.**
- **Returned** — Return to sender. Can update from delivered over time.
- **Failed** — Processing error. Contact DataSift support.

### Do
- Filter out Returned and Undeliverable in every preset
- Tag returned records so you can skip trace new addresses
- Toggle "Do Not Mail Ever" on records that request removal
- Check batch status 3-5 days after send date

### Don't
- Delete a template used in a scheduled/processing campaign
- Ignore Undeliverable records (they need fresh skip traces)
- Forget to exclude "Do Not Mail Ever" records (legal risk)
- Expect tracking on Real Penned letters (forever stamps bypass USPS tracking)

## Tracking External Mail (External Mailing Provider Workflow)

1. **Tag & Export** — Apply filter preset. Select All → Manage → Add Tags → "DM MM/YYYY" (e.g., DM 03/2026). Then Manage → Export Records.
2. **Send Externally** — Trigger campaign through Sift Mail, or upload CSV to external provider.
3. **Increment Attempts** — Back in DataSift: Select All from same filter → Manage → Update Attempts → increment Direct Mail by 1.

"For the 'Mail Monthly' external preset, add tags to exclude the current month's tag and include the previous month's tag. This prevents re-mailing records you just sent."

## Glossary

- **Real Penned Letter** — Machine-written using real ink. A6 envelope (Lavender/Navy Blue). Forever stamp. No USPS tracking. $1.79/piece on Business plan.
- **Snapback Check** — Mailer designed to look like a real check with soft offer amount. Postcard or self-mailer template with check-style layout. Re-engagement campaigns.
- **Multi-Touch Campaign** — Multiple different templates to same records over time, configurable delays.
- **Single Touch** — One mailer template. Send all at once or split into batches.
- **Batch Send** — Splitting single-touch campaign into smaller groups at intervals. Credits charged upfront.
- **Campaign Template** — Saved campaign config. **Business Plan only.**
- **Forever Stamp** — No tracking barcode. 1-3 day delivery (vs 5-7 presorted bulk). Used on Real Penned and Personal Letters.
- **Do Not Mail Ever** — Toggle on records that permanently excludes from all future mail.
- **DM Attempts** — Counter for times mailed. Auto-incremented when sending through DataSift.
- **Vacant Mailing** — Flag indicating mailing address is vacant. Always filter out (Params & Others → Vacant Mailing → No).
