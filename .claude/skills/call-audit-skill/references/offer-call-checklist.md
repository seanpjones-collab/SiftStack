# Offer Call Checklist — Evaluation Criteria

**Use for:** Offer Call, Renegotiation Call

---

## Checklist Steps & Scoring

### SECTION 1: Setting Expectations — Opening (0–10)
All 4 parts must be hit before presenting the offer:

**a. Time** — Did they confirm how much time the seller has?
**b. Agenda** — Did they lay out the agenda ("Here's what we'll cover today…")?
**c. Result** — Did they state the expected outcome ("By the end of this call we'll know if we can move forward…")?
**d. Permission to Say No** — Did they explicitly give the seller permission to say no with no pressure? ("My goal is to make sure you're 100% comfortable…")

---

### SECTION 2: What It Looks Like to Work With Us (0–10)
- Did they explain the process / what happens after they accept?
- Did they set expectations about what working together looks like (as-is, flexible closing, moving funds, occupancy, possessions, etc.)?
- Did they tailor this to the seller's specific situation and pain points?

---

### SECTION 3: Recap Motivation / Roadblocks / How We Can Help (0–10)
**This is the most critical section of the offer call.**

- Did they recap the seller's pain and situation before presenting price? ("Based on what you shared with me…")
- Did they connect the offer to the seller's specific pain points?
- Did they reference the seller's roadblocks and position the offer as the solution?
- Did they re-anchor the seller to their motivation (away from pain OR toward pleasure)?
- Did they leave room to show how WE specifically can help with their unique situation?

---

### SECTION 4: Present Offer (0–10)
- Was the offer presented with confidence and clarity?
- Did they state the number clearly without hedging or apologizing?
- Did they go SILENT after presenting the offer? (Critical — do not talk after giving the number)
- Did they frame the offer around the seller's outcome, not the property's value?

---

### SECTION 5: After Offer — Accepted or Not Accepted (0–10)

**If ACCEPTED:**
- Did they immediately move to lock it in? (Purchase agreement next steps)
- Did they confirm all terms verbally before sending the agreement?
- Did they set clear next steps and timeline?

**If NOT ACCEPTED — Objection Handling:**

**a. Go for No**
- Did they use the "Go for No" technique for vague/non-committal responses?
- Script: *"Hey [name], you've been very polite and I've enjoyed our conversation. With my experience, when someone tells me [___], it's usually just a polite way of saying no."*
- Did they box the objection after the seller corrected them?

**b. Box of Objections — did they identify WHICH objection it was?**
- Is it the idea of working with me? Did I do something wrong?
- Is it the process? Do you have questions about how this works?
- Is it the price?
- Are you considering other options?

**c. Go Back to Partner for Price**
- If price was the objection, did they use the "go back to partners" technique?
- Did they ask if there's a number in the middle they'd consider?
- Did they anchor a commitment: *"If I'm able to get them to approve [X], you're ready to move forward, right?"*

**Additional objection scripts to evaluate against:**
- "I need to think about it" → Box it: Which of the 4 objections is it really?
- "I need to talk to my attorney/realtor" → Set a deadline, confirm review timeline, schedule next call
- "I need to figure some things out first" → Box it
- Ghost / no-show → Re-engage text script ("manager came to me, we have additional funds…")
- Multiple offer situation → Premium funds script OR kick-the-can-down-the-road strategy

---

### SECTION 6: Notes (open)
- Any other standout moments, missed opportunities, or exceptional handling worth noting?

---

### Overall Call Score
Weight Sections 3 (Recap Motivation) and 5 (Objection Handling) most heavily — these are where deals are won or lost.

---

## Renegotiation-Specific Notes
For renegotiation calls, evaluate additionally:
- Did they re-establish rapport before bringing up the price change?
- Did they anchor the renegotiation to new information (inspection findings, comps, repair estimates)?
- Did they present the revised offer with the same confidence and silence technique?
- Did they use the objection handling scripts if pushback occurred?
- Did they avoid apologizing for the new number?
