## IDEAL CUSTOMER PROFILE (ICP)

### 9 Types of Ideal Sellers
1. **Owner Occupants** in distressed personal/property situations — downsizing, relocating
2. **Tired Landlords** — rental problems, aging out, accidental landlords (marriage/inheritance)
3. **Older/Aging Sellers** — property no longer fits needs (steps, maintenance, too big, financial pressure)
4. **Inherited/Probate Sellers** — unearned equity, property in disrepair, estate complications
5. **Tax-Delinquent Sellers** — financial + situational distress, facing tax sale
6. **Impatient or Procrastinating Sellers** — truly NEED to sell but stalling
7. **Code Violation Sellers** — harassment from codes authorities, can't fix it themselves
8. **Vacant Property Sellers** — paying taxes/insurance with no benefit, liability exposure
9. **Sellers with Major Repair Needs** — can't afford or don't want to deal with repairs

### How We Help (Solutions to offer in the call)
- Take as-is
- Flexible closing date
- Moving funds before closing
- Occupancy after closing during transition
- Leave possessions behind
- Handle tenants/evictions
- Spread tax liability over time
- Deal with codes directly
- Work through estate complications

## CHAMP QUALIFICATION FRAMEWORK

Score 0–8 across 4 categories (0 = No Fit, 1 = Workable, 2 = Complete):

| Category | 0 pts | 1 pt | 2 pts |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Challenges** | No challenges, seller in no pain, good condition | Some challenges, minor repairs, vacant, two houses | Clear challenges: major repairs, no heat/AC, behind on mortgage/taxes, bad tenants, health issues |
| **Authority** | Person can't sell, not an influencer | Some authority (one of multiple owners/heirs) or is an influencer | Full authority to sell; all decision-makers ideally at appointment |
| **Money (Equity)** | No equity — DEAD DEAL | ~25%+ equity | 50%+ equity or fully equitized |
| **Priority** | No need to sell, not going to sell any time soon | Wants to sell within 90 days | NEEDS to sell within 30 days |

**Scoring:**
- 0–2: Dead/Cold — Not a fit
- 3–4: Warm — Possible fit, @mention Lead Manager
- 5–6: Warm/Hot — Probable fit, schedule appointment
- 7–8: A-List — Dream seller, always an appointment

### Customer Tiers
- **A-List**: Dream sellers — clear challenges, NEED to sell, have equity, in buying area. ALWAYS appointment.
- **Bread & Butter**: In the sweet spot — some challenges, some equity, open to selling. USUALLY appointment.
- **Change of Situation/Compelling Event**: Life-changing event drove them to sell. Often hot priority. USUALLY appointment if willing.
- **Dead Ends**: No equity OR no challenges and price-fixed. Cold priority. NEVER appointment.

## MOTIVATION CATEGORIES

The specialist should identify WHICH type of pain the seller has and whether they are moving:
- **Away from Pain** (escaping a bad situation), or
- **Toward Pleasure** (moving toward something better)

**Pain Types to Probe:**
1. **Financial** — debt, behind on mortgage, taxes, foreclosure
2. **Emotional** — stress, anxiety, overwhelm, attachment
3. **Relationship** — divorce, family disputes, inheritance disagreements, co-owner conflict
4. **Physical** — property in disrepair, health/mobility concerns
5. **Mental** — overwhelmed by the process, indecision, fatigue
6. **Time** — property is consuming too much of their time
7. **Blocked Goal** — property is preventing them from doing something else
8. **Extreme Pleasure Missed** — stuck in current situation, missing out on better life
9. **No Motivation** — Red flag; probe to find a compelling event or close as dead

**Impact Question Framework (at least 2–3 levels deep):**
- Level 1: "What has that been like for you?" / "How long has this been going on?"
- Level 2: "How has that been affecting [other areas of their life]?"
- Level 3: "What happens if this doesn't get resolved?"
- Perfect Picture: "What would the perfect outcome look like for you?"

## REBUTTAL SHEET — KEY SCRIPTS

### Uncovering Influencers
"Have you spoken with [spouse/family/attorney/realtor] about the idea of selling?"
- Yes → proceed to motivation questions
- No → send them with homework to discuss first

For attorneys/realtors: Acknowledge, set deadline on offer, confirm review timeline, schedule next call.

### Getting a Number (3 Attempts)
1. "Based on everything you told me, what do you feel is a fair price for the property?"
2. "Obviously you've thought about what you'd want to walk away with, right?"
3. "I'm worried that if I don't have a number and I go back to my partners, my partners will set the price — and I'm afraid it'll be a price you don't like."

### Go for No
*When seller gives non-committal response:*
"Hey [name], you've been very polite and I've enjoyed our conversation. With my experience, usually when someone tells me [X], it's really just a polite way of saying no."
→ If they correct you, you've boxed the objection. Now handle it.

### Box of Objections
After an offer is made and not accepted, identify which:
1. Is it the idea of working with me? Did I do something wrong?
2. Is it the process?
3. Is it the price?
4. Are you considering other options?

### Offer Not Accepted — Price Objection
"I can appreciate that. I can't guarantee anything, but if I go back to my partners and see if we can do better — is there a number somewhere in the middle you'd consider? / If I'm able to get them to approve [their price], you're definitely ready to move forward, right?"

### Re-Engage After Ghost
"Hey [name], my manager just came to me and said we have additional funds to allocate this week. They told me we can actually pay you more for your property. When's a good time today to hop on a call?"

### Virtual Withdrawal
"Do you have a pen and paper? Write this down. This is the virtual withdrawal account number we have these funds set aside in. It's only good for 24 hours. VW-1234567."

### Multiple Offer Situation
- **Option 1 — Kick the can**: Position yourself as last and best offer. Call back at deadline, ask for competing numbers, go in to fight for seller.
- **Option 2 — Premium funds**: Go in near max offer, take control, eliminate competition, negotiate later if needed.

## LEAD STATUS DISPOSITION

| Status | When to Use |
|---|---|
| Discovery | Any call with no contact or no scheduled callback |
| Add to Follow Up | Not interested now, or wants to sell 6+ months out |
| Do Not Call | Seller explicitly requests removal |
| Dead | Not homeowner, bad/fake info, duplicate, fraudulent |
| Interested Add to FU | Interested but not ready; offer made but not accepted |
| Interested → Set Offer Status | Process done, in underwriting, awaiting offer call |
| In Contract | Signed contract |
| Refer to Agent | Wants retail; push to agent workspace, set 30-day follow-up |

**Key rules:**
- Offers not accepted → follow up every 7 days for 10 weeks minimum
- New untouched leads → call within 10 minutes, hit multiple times
- Interested Set Offer leads → only assigned ACQ calls these (never open dialer)

## RED FLAG INDICATORS (Coaching/Management)

### Key KPIs to Watch
- **Offers per contract ratio**: Target 2–3 offers per deal
- **Process per offer ratio (Golden Rule)**: 1 offer for every 2 processes
- **Calls over 6 minutes**: Indicator of quality conversations
- **Ghosting rate**: Red flag for not setting proper expectations / non-committal language
- **Projected profit vs. goals**: Is the team hitting profit targets?

### Common Root Causes
- Team doing deal analysis BEFORE calling (causes long breaks between calls)
- Not spending time with qualified leads; wasting time on dead ends
- Not setting next steps or scheduling follow-ups properly
- Non-committal language not being addressed with Go for No
- Influencers not being uncovered
- UMBC not being checked on follow-up calls

## ACQUISITION PROCESS CHECKLIST (Post-Call Steps)
After a successful process call, the ACQ specialist should:
1. Click "Process Call Done" button in Podio; turn off nurture campaign
2. Look up property value on Zillow for pricing gauge
3. Run comps → determine conservative ARV → save CMA in Podio
4. Determine repair estimate (from call info or seller photos)
5. Determine offer using MAO formula or deal analyser spreadsheet
6. Review offer with leadership (send: address, roof/HVAC/HWH age, foundation type, septic/well, ARV, repairs, MAO)
7. Call back → give presentation → make offer
8. Fill out and send Purchase Agreement
9. Review PA with seller (6 most important parts)
10. Get SIGNED Purchase Agreement → Ring the Gong
