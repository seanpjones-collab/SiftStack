# Voice Guide

Write like a helpful friend who is direct, practical, and doesn't add fluff. Every sentence should have a purpose. Keep it simple enough that a 5th grader could read and understand it.

---

## Reading Level: 5th Grade

All content must be written at a **5th-grade reading level**. This makes the content easy to read and understand without watering down the ideas.

**How to Write at a 5th-Grade Level:**

| Do This | Not This |
|---------|----------|
| Use short sentences (under 20 words) | Long, complex sentences with multiple clauses |
| Use common, everyday words | Jargon, technical terms, or fancy words |
| One idea per sentence | Multiple ideas packed into one sentence |
| Active voice ("Click the button") | Passive voice ("The button should be clicked") |

**Word Swaps:**

| Replace This | With This |
|--------------|-----------|
| utilize | use |
| implement | set up, start |
| leverage | use |
| optimize | improve |
| facilitate | help |
| comprehensive | complete, full |
| subsequently | then, next |
| methodology | method, way |
| functionality | feature |
| acquisition | getting, buying |
| prioritize | focus on |
| maximize | get the most from |
| systematically | step by step |

**Test Your Writing:**

Before finishing any section, ask: "Would a 10-year-old understand this?" If not, simplify it.

---

## Core Principles

**Be direct.** Say things plainly. Don't hedge or over-explain.

**Be practical.** Focus on what to do and why it matters. Skip the theory unless it helps.

**Be specific.** Use real numbers, times, and examples instead of vague words.

**Be natural.** Write the way you'd explain something to a friend.

---

## What to Do

| Principle | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Talk to the reader directly | "You'll spend most of your time hunting." |
| Explain why things matter | "Hunting is more important than killing because you can't do one without the other." |
| Use specific numbers | "Review call notes the night before and the morning of your calls." |
| Keep sentences short | "Let's break down each one." |
| Move on after making your point | Don't repeat what you just said |

---

## What to Avoid

| Avoid | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Meta-language ("the metaphor is...", "this framework...") | Just say the concept directly |
| Signature phrases ("Here's the thing...") | They get repetitive |
| Warning patterns ("I've seen too many people...") | Just say what to do and what not to do |
| Over-explaining what you're about to explain | Get to the point |
| Filler transitions ("Furthermore...", "Additionally...") | Use simple words or just start the next sentence |
| Repeating what was just said | Trust the reader to get it the first time |
| Big words when small words work | Keep it simple |

---

## Introducing Concepts

Don't announce concepts. Just say them.

**Avoid:**
> "The metaphor we use here is Hunt Mode vs Kill Mode. This metaphor helps you understand the two types of activities..."

**Use:**
> "Closers work in two modes: Hunt Mode and Kill Mode. Hunt Mode is everything you do to get people on the phone. Kill Mode is everything you do on the phone to close the sale."

---

## Transitions

Keep transitions simple.

| Instead of | Use |
|------------|-----|
| "Subsequently..." | "Then..." or "Next..." |
| "Furthermore..." | Just start the next sentence |
| "It is important to note that..." | Say it directly |
| "In conclusion..." | Don't conclude—just end |

---

## Explaining Processes

When explaining a process:

1. Say what the process does (one sentence)
2. Break it into steps
3. For each step, say what to do and why
4. Move on

Don't add lead-ins about what you're about to explain. Don't add summaries repeating what you just explained.

---

## Examples of Good Writing

**Direct and practical:**
> "Call notes help you close more deals. If you know your prospect, you know what offer works best for them and how to handle their objections."

**Simple concept introduction:**
> "Closers have two goals: Get More Opportunities and Close More Deals. More Opportunities × Better Closing = More Sales."

**Natural flow:**
> "Hunting has four parts: Call Notes, Working Your List, Outbound, and End of Day Checklist. Let's break down each one."
